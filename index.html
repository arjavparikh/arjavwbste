MTV Nano Drive [Contestant|2013]

Nano Drive with MTV was one of it's kind Digital show where travel enthusiasts travel across India every year for 21 days in 4 Tata Nano's across 4 zones of India; North East West & South.The teams have to survive the day with a mere budget of Rs. 4000 for food, fuel and stay for 4 people in the team.Across 21 days, every day, a full edited video is uploaded and shared on the MTV Networks. The contestants share photographs live from the journey. Points are given to each team based on the number of number of views their videos get, the number of likes, tweets and their social interaction online. India Film Project [2014 - Present]

Started as an intern I have been associated with the team till now. India Film Project is Asia's largest online and on-ground competition which encourages a film buff to make a film over the weekend, away from your huff-buff schedule, and showcase it to people around and get awarded.
With IFP I started my journey as an intern and had always been a support whenever needed. Gradually I merged more into the team and started helping in various aspects of the project. My major contribution remains in managing the flow of the project.
This year (2016) more than 15000 filmakerfs from 700 plus teams participated, every year the participation increases. To figure out an efficient way for the registration process, communication channel as well as the film submissions is a task. I designed a very systematic solution using many different tools for the same.
A glimpse of the process I designed has been penned down below:

  • Registration process was shifted from "Google Forms" to custom made registration form on the website which was linked to a SQL database.
  • An excel sheet was updated at regular intervals from the database to add new participants who register. This sheet was useful to the communication team.
  • For communication with the participants SMS and bulk emailers were used.
  • The films were accepted over "Airdrop" which was link to an email ID. A script was written to automatically start download from Airdrop as soon as a film was received.

MIT Media Labs India Initiative [Volunteer| January 2014]

The Media Lab India Initiative is an effort by students and alumni from the MIT Media Lab to bring together a community of makers and creators across India, and to facilitate collaboration and sharing of ideas across boundaries of disciplines, institutions and culture. The goal is to create an environment much like at the MIT Media Lab, an environment that fosters creative exploration of ideas. Over the past four years, MIT Media Lab India Initiative organized four workshops in Pune, Delhi, Ahmedabad and Bangalore. Each five-day workshop brings together hundreds of students with backgrounds in art, science, technology and design to collaborate in teams and to come up with prototypes and design concepts, which are showcased in an open house at the end of the workshop. Each workshop consists of a number of tracks or focus areas, conducted by two instructors from the Media Lab who guide about 50 students through the process of observing, brainstorming, and prototyping.

I was the organizer of the Ahmedabad workshop which was from 16th January 2015 to 23 January 2015.
My role included:

  • Managing application entries.
  • Managing promotional strategies for bringing more entries.

  • Coordinating with students and faculties and the venue administration.
  • Venue management.
  • Managing to set up the infrastructure at the venue.
  • Managing volunteers.
  • Look after event itinerary and make sure everything executes on time.

    A Thousand Eyes

    A thousand eyes is an electronic garment I am currently making (as on 1 December 2016) for a fashion show in Canada.

    The garment takes its colour story from the vibrant peacock colours. Furthur more the prints and the danglers are an inspiration from peacocks feathers and the way the open up during rains. The light effects on the surface would further add to the peacock’s shimmering resembelance.

    The fabric used the garment would be majorly viscose or dual tone silk to achieve the natural shimmering effect of a peacock’s body while maintaining a soft appearance. The shoulder pieces and danglers would be backed with buckrum or high density fusing to provide a stiff appearance. LED would be incorporated by creating sections and piping on the insides of the garment, they would further be backed by foam layers or other mediums to give the LEDs a diffusing effect. The surface will consist of ornamentation techniques such as digital prints and some handworked areas by artisans.
